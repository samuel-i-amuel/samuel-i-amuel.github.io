About Me

Hi! My name is Sam Stockbridge. I'm an award-winning data journalist, investigative reporter, graphics developer and occasional photojournalist. Welcome to my website!

I built this website from scratch with HTML and CSS a few weeks ago, so please excuse the rough edges. I'm working on expanding it to showcase some of my photography and host interactive graphics once I develop some.

For the past six months I’ve been covering the Texas Legislature for Reform Austin, with a focus on public schools and gun violence. I followed the arc of Texas private school subsidies from bill to law, and the fight for additional public school funding. I also tracked the election of the Texas Democratic Party chair, reporting on the details of a legal challenge to the top candidate’s eligibility.

Before I moved to Texas, I lived in Ketchikan, Alaska, for four-and-a-half years, reporting on local government for the Ketchikan Daily News. I covered about 200 meetings of the Ketchikan Gateway Borough Assembly and the Ketchikan School Board, writing them up on a tight deadline to be the community’s definitive account of what happened. I also followed municipal and state elections and the Alaska Marine Highway System.

I got my real start in computer-assisted reporting to investigate campaign finance discrepancies in the race for the state House District 36 race in Alaska, reporting that earned me an Alaska Press Club award for political coverage. In two weeks, I wrote a Python script to scrape the Alaska Political Offices Commission website for finance data, then clean it and generate summaries of the contributions before rendering graphics of the result.

I used that same script as a framework to illuminate the nuances of the 2022 midterm elections in Alaska, work that earned me another award, this time for multimedia presentation.

In 2024, I switched to a different methodology to produce those graphics directly from the state’s csv files, and used that to deepen my understanding of the state finance to produce summaries of every candidate in the election and generate spreadsheets containing particularly high-value targets.

When I’m not writing stories, you might find me at local art galleries, birding on a nature trail, riding my favorite bike, volunteering with my local Rotary club or playing my flute in the park.
